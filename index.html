WHO IS JESSICA COX?

Jessica Cox was born without arms. The doctors do not know why she was born 'differently-abled.' But she does. Jessica has made achievements with her feet that most people couldn't imagine. Now she shares her story with people around the world.

Born in 1983 in Sierra Vista, Arizona, Jessica has learned to live her life with her feet. There were many questions at the time about whether Jessica would be able to live a 'normal' life. However, Jessica's father has said he never dropped a tear about her birth condition. He had full confidence in her potential. With the support of her parents and family, Jessica became confident in herself as an adult, and continued to explore the world with her feet.

제시카 콕스는 누구인가?

제시카 콕스는 팔이 없이 태어났다. 의사들은 왜 그녀가 '다른 능력을 가지고' 태어났는지 알지 못한다. 하지만 그녀는 안다. 제시카는 대부분의 사람들이 상상조차 할 수 없는 일들을 자신의 발로 이루어 왔다. 이제 그녀는 자신의 이야기를 전 세계 사람들과 나누고 있다.

1983년 애리조나주 시에라 비스타에서 태어난 제시카는 발로 자신의 삶을 살아가는 법을 배워왔다. 당시 제시카가 '정상적인' 삶을 살 수 있을지에 대해 많은 의문이 제기되었다. 그러나 제시카의 아버지는 딸의 출생 상태에 대해 단 한 번도 눈물을 흘린 적이 없다고 말했다. 그는 딸의 잠재력에 대해 완전한 확신을 가지고 있었다. 부모와 가족의 지원 속에서, 제시카는 성인이 되어 자신감 있는 사람으로 성장했고, 발로 세상을 계속해서 탐험해 나갔다.

主주제 (Topic)

팔 없이 태어났지만 발로 비범한 성취를 이뤄낸 제시카 콕스의 삶과 자신감의 형성

旨요지 (Main Idea)

신체적 한계는 인생의 가능성을 결정짓지 않으며, 가족의 무한한 지지와 자기 자신에 대한 믿음이 제시카를 '다른 능력'을 가진 한 사람으로 성장시켰다.

Dance, Taekwondo, and Psychology

춤, 태권도, 심리학 — 도전의 연속

As a child, Jessica studied dance in her town. When the first performance arrived, she asked to be put in the back row. Her dance teacher told her there was no back row. Tentatively, she took the stage with the other students and performed her routine. When she finished, the huge applause from the audience gave her the encouragement and confidence to continue dancing for 14 years.

Jessica's parents eventually met a Taekwondo instructor named Jim Cunningham. His response when told of her birth condition was that she would be more than physically able and that only her attitude could hold her back. At the age of 14, Jessica earned her first black belt in the International Taekwondo Federation.

After graduating from high school, Jessica majored in psychology in university. She frequently explains that psychology believes the way people think has a greater impact on their lives than a physical limitation.

During college, Jessica found an ATA Martial Arts club and resumed training in the sport of Taekwondo. Even though Jessica already had one black belt in a different style, she had to relearn the material for all belt colors. The instructors created a curriculum that would be accessible to any future armless students. Jessica then became the first armless person to earn a black belt in the ATA.

Think Outside the Shoe

Hi, my name is Jessica Cox. As you may know, I'm the first armless person to have obtained a pilot's license. When I climbed into the airplane on the first day flight training, I was faced with the challenge of buckling my safety belt. As I sat there thinking this through, it reminded me of a challenge on the elementary school playground, learning how to tie my shoelaces. Unlike all the other children who can tie their laces with their feet inside the shoe, I had to keep both feet out of my shoe so that I could tie the laces with my toes. At first, I tied the laces too tight to put the shoes on. After several trials and errors, I came to realize that I should tie them loose enough to slip my feet in. Even though it seemed a small challenge, the most important thing I learned from that was how to 'think outside the shoe.'

Remembering my experience back in the old days, I immediately stood up on the airplane seat, buckled the harness, loosened the straps and slipped into the seat belt. Yet this was only the beginning of challenges of flight training. Over the course of two years, the difficulties of safely reaching the controls with my feet prevented me from moving on to the next stage, solo flight. I went a solid year without flight training. I felt that everyone had already given up on me. It was a very low moment, but was also the moment that I realized if I wanted this badly enough I would find a way. After all, my life is about finding a way.

I remember one thing about a TV. When I moved out of my parents' house into a flat near my college, my roommate and I could not afford to get a TV. I decided to borrow one from my parents.

Persistence Makes the Impossible Possible

Solving a problem requires persistence. You have to keep working at it. Do not give up at the first sign of failure—or the second, or third, or fourth or fifth. Be persistent. Anne Sullivan, who was Helen Keller's teacher, lost her sight and was left in an institution as a young child. Yet, she had persistence, and she wanted an education despite her disability and station in life. Her persistence eventually led her to spend several years teaching one of the worlds' most famous deaf and blind people how to communicate.

Likewise, persistence has helped me to overcome adversity. For example, after the doctor told my family I would never be able to drive, my driving instructor told me maybe I could drive with the right equipment. He gave me the name of a medical equipment company. After following up with it, I found a California-based dealer who sold adapted vans, which I could drive from my wheel chair. The van had controls that could be adapted to my needs and abilities. Even after I had the vehicle, more adjustments were necessary before everything worked correctly. As a result of persistence, I am now on my sixth van and have driven nearly one-half million miles. I often drive the van to speaking engagements.

Persistence was also involved in my career choice. I had to try several different positions until I found something that fit me. This persistence eventually led to motivational speaking. Had I listened to the doctor with the dreaded results, I might never have tried to work at all—or I might have tried one or two jobs and then given up. I may never have lived to hear the youth say to me, "Cuz, you're not done yet."

문제를 해결하려면 끈기가 필요하다. 계속 그것에 매달려야 한다. 첫 번째 실패의 징후에 — 두 번째에도, 세 번째에도, 네 번째 또는 다섯 번째에도 — 포기하지 말아야 한다. 끈기 있는 사람이 되라. 헬렌 켈러의 선생님이었던 앤 설리번은 어린 시절 시력을 잃고 시설에 남겨졌다. 그러나 그녀는 끈기를 가지고 있었으며, 자신의 장애와 인생의 처지에도 불구하고 교육을 받기를 원했다. 그녀의 끈기는 결국 그녀를 세계에서 가장 유명한 청각·시각 장애인 중 한 사람에게 의사소통하는 법을 가르치는 데 수년을 바치게 했다.

이와 마찬가지로, 끈기는 내가 역경을 극복하도록 도와주었다. 예를 들어, 의사가 우리 가족에게 내가 결코 운전할 수 없을 것이라고 말한 후에, 운전 강사는 적절한 장비만 있다면 어쩌면 내가 운전할 수도 있을 것이라고 말해 주었다. 그는 한 의료 기기 회사의 이름을 알려 주었다. 후속 조치를 취한 끝에 나는 캘리포니아에 본사를 둔 한 판매상을 찾았는데, 그곳은 휠체어에서 운전할 수 있는 개조된 밴을 팔고 있었다. 그 밴에는 나의 필요와 능력에 맞게 조절될 수 있는 조종 장치가 있었다. 차량을 가진 후에도 모든 것이 제대로 작동하려면 더 많은 조정이 필요했다. 끈기의 결과로 나는 지금 여섯 번째 밴을 타고 있고, 거의 50만 마일 가까이를 운전해 왔다. 나는 종종 그 밴을 몰고 강연 약속에 간다.

끈기는 나의 직업 선택에도 관여했다. 나는 나에게 맞는 일을 찾을 때까지 여러 다른 직책들을 시도해 보아야 했다. 이 끈기가 결국 동기부여 강연으로 이어졌다. 만약 그 무서운 예측을 한 의사의 말을 그대로 들었더라면, 나는 아예 일을 시도조차 하지 않았거나 — 한두 가지 일을 해 보고 그만두었을지도 모른다. 어쩌면 나는 살아서 한 청년이 나에게 "사촌 형, 형은 아직 끝난 게 아니에요."라고 말하는 것을 듣지 못했을지도 모른다.
